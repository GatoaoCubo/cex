# Safety Policy

## Purpose
Establish organizational AI safety governance rules to mitigate risks, ensure ethical use, and protect stakeholders.

## Scope
Applies to all AI systems, data processing, and decision-making processes within the organization.

## Principles
1. **Transparency**: Document AI system capabilities, limitations, and decision-making processes.
2. **Accountability**: Assign clear ownership for AI system safety and performance.
3. **Fairness**: Ensure AI systems do not discriminate and operate equitably.
4. **Security**: Protect data and systems from unauthorized access and breaches.
5. **Compliance**: Adhere to legal, regulatory, and ethical standards.

## Governance Structure
- **AI Safety Committee**: Oversees policy implementation and audits.
- **Data Stewards**: Manage data quality, privacy, and security.
- **Ethics Review Board**: Evaluates AI system ethical implications.

## Risk Management
- Conduct regular risk assessments for AI systems.
- Implement mitigation strategies for identified risks.
- Monitor system performance and update policies as needed.

## Compliance
- All AI systems must undergo safety certification before deployment.
- Regular audits and penetration testing are required.
- Non-compliance may result in project suspension or termination.

## Incident Response
- Establish protocols for addressing safety incidents, biases, or failures.
- Define escalation paths for urgent safety concerns.
- Maintain records of incidents and corrective actions.

## Continuous Improvement
- Review and update safety policies annually.
- Incorporate feedback from audits, incidents, and stakeholder input.
- Invest in employee training on AI safety best practices.

## Appendices
- **A. Regulatory Framework**: List applicable laws and standards.
- **B. Incident Reporting Template**: Standardized form for safety incidents.
- **C. Glossary**: Definitions of key terms (AI safety, risk mitigation, etc.).

## Domain Overview  
Case studies are narrative-driven artifacts that document real-world challenges, solutions, and outcomes to demonstrate value, validate approaches, or guide decision-making. They are widely used in technology, healthcare, and business sectors to communicate complex problem-solving journeys. Effective case studies follow a structured narrative (challenge → solution → outcome) and include qualitative/quantitative metrics, stakeholder quotes, and contextual details to ensure credibility and actionable insights.  

The industry emphasizes rigor in data representation, ethical considerations (e.g., anonymizing sensitive information), and alignment with frameworks like the Harvard Business Review case study methodology. Case studies often serve as evidence for product efficacy, process improvements, or strategic shifts, requiring balance between storytelling and technical accuracy.  

## Key Concepts  
| Concept                | Definition                                                                 | Source                          |  
|-----------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------|  
| Problem Statement     | Clear articulation of the challenge faced by the client or organization.   | ISO 26514:2016                   |  
| Solution Architecture | Technical or procedural framework used to address the problem.             | IEEE 1220-2013                   |  
| Outcome Metrics       | Quantifiable results (e.g., ROI, efficiency gains) from the solution.      | Harvard Business Review         |  
| Stakeholder Quote     | Direct testimony from a client or team member validating the solution.     | ACM Case Study Template         |  
| Contextual Framing    | Background information to situate the case within industry trends.         | OECD Guidelines for Case Studies|  
| Risk Mitigation       | Strategies to address potential failures or unintended consequences.       | ISO 31000:2018                   |  
| Benchmarking          | Comparison against industry standards or competitors’ performance.         | Gartner Research Framework      |  
| Ethical Disclosure    | Transparency about limitations, biases, or unresolved issues.              | ACM SIGCHI Ethics Guidelines    |  

## Industry Standards  
- ISO 26514:2016 (Case study methodology for technical fields)  
- IEEE 1220-2013 (Software engineering case study guidelines)  
- OECD Guidelines for Multinational Enterprises (ethical context)  
- Gartner Hype Cycle for Emerging Technologies (benchmarking trends)  
- ISO 31000:2018 (Risk management frameworks)  
- ACM SIGCHI Ethics Guidelines (for human-centric case studies)  
- RFC 7991 (Documentation standards for technical narratives)  
- COBIT 2019 (IT governance frameworks for solution validation)  

## Common Patterns  
1. **Challenge-first narrative** – Open with the problem to hook readers.  
2. **Data-driven solution** – Use metrics to justify the approach taken.  
3. **Quote integration** – Embed stakeholder feedback to add credibility.  
4. **Before/after contrast** – Highlight transformation through visual or textual comparison.  
5. **Lessons learned** – Conclude with actionable takeaways for the audience.  

## Pitfalls  
- Overhyping outcomes without supporting data.  
- Omitting contextual details that limit generalizability.  
- Using biased language that skews the narrative.  
- Failing to anonymize sensitive client information.  
- Ignoring industry standards, leading to reduced credibility.

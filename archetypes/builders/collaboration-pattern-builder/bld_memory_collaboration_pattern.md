## Observation  
Common issues include ambiguous role definitions leading to conflicting agent behaviors, and insufficient alignment on shared goals causing miscoordination. Overly rigid topologies often fail to adapt to dynamic environments.  

## Pattern  
Effective patterns emphasize clear role hierarchies or peer-to-peer symmetry, paired with explicit rules for conflict resolution. Modular topologies that allow localized coordination without global dependencies improve scalability.  

## Evidence  
Reviewed artifacts showed peer-to-peer patterns in logistics systems reduced bottlenecks, while central coordinator patterns in healthcare improved consistency during emergencies.  

## Recommendations  
- Define agent roles and responsibilities with unambiguous boundaries.  
- Use modular topologies to isolate coordination scopes and minimize cascading failures.  
- Embed conflict resolution mechanisms (e.g., voting, priority rules) into the pattern.  
- Simulate coordination under stress scenarios to validate topology robustness.  
- Document assumptions about environmental dynamics and agent capabilities.

## Domain Overview  
Organizational AI safety governance rules define structured approaches to mitigate risks from AI systems throughout their lifecycle. These policies address ethical, technical, and operational risks, ensuring alignment with organizational values and stakeholder expectations. Key focus areas include risk tolerance thresholds, safety assurance processes, and incident response protocols. Unlike compliance frameworks, safety policies prioritize proactive risk management over regulatory adherence, often integrating principles from ethical AI, human-centric design, and technical robustness. Industry leaders increasingly adopt safety policies to balance innovation with accountability, as seen in frameworks like IEEE 7000 and ISO/IEC 23894.  

Safety governance rules are distinct from threat modeling, which focuses on adversarial risks, and compliance frameworks, which map to legal requirements. Instead, safety policies emphasize internal controls, such as validation protocols, oversight mechanisms, and transparency requirements. They often involve cross-functional teams, including technical experts, ethicists, and legal advisors, to ensure holistic risk mitigation. As AI systems grow in complexity, safety policies are critical for maintaining trust and preventing unintended harm.

## Common Patterns  
1. Cross-functional oversight committees for policy enforcement.  
2. Risk-based prioritization of safety controls.  
3. Embedding safety by design into AI development workflows.  
4. Regular third-party audits for policy compliance.  
5. Stakeholder engagement for policy alignment.  
6. Feedback loops for continuous policy refinement.  

## Pitfalls  
- Overly broad policies that lack actionable guidelines.  
- Ignoring technical feasibility during policy drafting.  
- Failing to align policies with organizational objectives.  
- Underestimating the need for human oversight in AI decisions.  
- Not updating policies to reflect evolving AI capabilities or risks.

The flywheel order is as follows:
Engineering (N03) -> Knowledge (N04) -> Research (N01) -> Operations (N05) -> Admin (N07) -> Marketing (N02) -> Commercial (N06)

This order ensures that the nuclei are fed in a continuous cycle, with each nucleus building on the output of the previous one.

The flywheel order is designed to optimize the flow of information and resources between the different nuclei. The Engineering nucleus builds the knowledge infrastructure, which is then used by the Knowledge nucleus to generate insights. These insights are then used by the Research nucleus to inform decision-making, while the Operations nucleus uses them to drive business outcomes. The Admin nucleus provides support and maintenance for the flywheel, while the Marketing nucleus promotes the output of the flywheel to external stakeholders. Finally, the Commercial nucleus monetizes the output of the flywheel.

This order is based on the assumption that the nuclei are interconnected and that each nucleus builds on the output of the previous one. It is designed to maximize the efficiency and effectiveness of the flywheel, while minimizing waste and duplication of effort.

The flywheel order can be adjusted as needed to accommodate changes in the business or market environment. However, it is essential to maintain a consistent order to ensure that the nuclei are fed in a continuous cycle and that the output of each nucleus is used effectively by the next one.

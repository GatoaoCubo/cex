  Customer segment/ICP definition artifact with firmographics and needs

  A customer segment represents a group of customers with similar characteristics, needs, and behaviors. The Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) is a detailed description of the perfect customer for a product or service. This artifact defines:
  
  1. **Firmographics**: 
     - Company size (employees, revenue, market cap)
     - Industry vertical (e.g., healthcare, fintech)
     - Geographic location (regions, countries)
     - Technological maturity (early-stage, growth, mature)
  
  2. **Needs**: 
     - Pain points (e.g., operational inefficiencies, compliance risks)
     - Goals (e.g., scaling operations, improving customer retention)
     - Decision-making criteria (e.g., ROI thresholds, implementation timelines)
  
  3. **Behavioral patterns**: 
     - Buying cycle length (short-term vs. long-term)
     - Preferred communication channels (email, LinkedIn, in-person)
     - Budget allocation for solutions
  
  This foundational artifact guides targeting strategies, marketing messaging, and product development priorities. It serves as the basis for personalized outreach and resource allocation in sales and marketing operations.

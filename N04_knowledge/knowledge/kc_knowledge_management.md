## Key Concepts
- **Pythagorean Theorem**: In right triangles, \( a^2 + b^2 = c^2 \), where \( c \) is hypotenuse.
- **Right Triangle**: A triangle with one angle measuring 90 degrees.
- **Hypotenuse**: The longest side in a right triangle, opposite the right angle.

## Strategy Phases
1. **Identification**: Confirm a 90-degree angle in the triangle.
2. **Application**: Use \( a^2 + b^2 = c^2 \) to solve for unknown sides.
3. **Validation**: Cross-check calculations with context or diagram.

## Golden Rules
- ALWAYS check triangle type before using the theorem.
- NEVER apply outside Euclidean geometry unless modified version is proven.
- ALWAYS label sides clearly to avoid mistakes in calculations.

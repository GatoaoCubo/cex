**Model Architecture** refers to the structural design of an artificial neural network, defining how data flows through layers of interconnected nodes (neurons) and the mathematical operations performed at each layer.

Key components include:
- **Input/Output Layers**: Define data dimensions and transformation goals
- **Hidden Layers**: Process intermediate representations (e.g., dense, convolutional, recurrent)
- **Activation Functions**: Introduce non-linearity (e.g., ReLU, sigmoid, tanh)
- **Optimization Techniques**: Weight initialization, regularization, normalization
- **Connectivity Patterns**: Fully connected, sparse, or hierarchical connections

Common architectures:
1. **Feedforward Neural Networks** (dense layers)
2. **Convolutional Neural Networks** (CNNs) for spatial data
3. **Recurrent Neural Networks** (RNNs/LSTMs) for sequential data
4. **Transformers** (self-attention mechanisms)
5. **Hybrid architectures** combining multiple paradigms

Critical considerations:
- Computational efficiency vs. representational capacity
- Training dynamics and convergence properties
- Generalization ability across domains
- Interpretability and model explainability

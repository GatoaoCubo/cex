# Subscription Tier Definition

A subscription tier is a pricing model that defines access levels, features, and cost structures for SaaS products. Tiers typically differentiate by:

## Pricing Models
- **Monthly**: Recurring payments every 30 days
- **Yearly**: Annual subscription with potential discounts
- **Pay-as-you-go**: Usage-based pricing without fixed terms

## Feature Matrix
| Feature          | Basic Tier | Professional Tier | Enterprise Tier |
|------------------|------------|-------------------|------------------|
| Storage         | 10GB       | 100GB            | Unlimited        |
| Users           | 1          | 10               | 50               |
| Support         | Email      | Priority         | 24/7 Dedicated   |
| Analytics       | Limited    | Standard         | Custom Reports   |
| API Access      | No         | Basic            | Full             |
| SLA             | N/A        | 99.9%            | 99.99%           |

## Key Considerations
1. **Value Proposition**: Tiers must clearly communicate feature differences
2. **Pricing Strategy**: Align pricing with perceived value and market positioning
3. **Scalability**: Ensure tiers accommodate business growth needs
4. **Compliance**: Maintain transparent pricing and feature disclosure

Example: A SaaS CRM might offer:
- **Free Tier**: 1 user, 10GB storage, basic analytics
- **Pro Tier**: 10 users, 100GB storage, advanced reporting
- **Enterprise Tier**: 50 users, unlimited storage, custom SLAs

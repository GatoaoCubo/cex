# Conformity Assessment for High-Risk AI Systems (EU AI Act Annex IV)

## Overview
Conformity assessment is a mandatory process for high-risk AI systems under the EU AI Act (Article 43), with a compliance deadline of August 2026. This process verifies that AI systems meet the legal requirements for safety, transparency, and human oversight.

## Key Requirements
- **Risk classification**: Systems must be classified as high-risk based on their potential impact
- **Technical documentation**: Detailed records of design, development, and testing
- **CE marking**: Mandatory conformity declaration with technical documentation
- **Third-party verification**: Independent validation by accredited bodies for certain systems
- **Data governance**: Compliance with data protection and transparency requirements

## Assessment Phases
1. **Preparation**: Document technical specifications and risk analysis
2. **Testing**: Validate system compliance with AI Act requirements
3. **Verification**: Third-party audit and certification
4. **Monitoring**: Ongoing compliance checks post-deployment

## Compliance Deadline
All high-risk AI systems must complete conformity assessment by **August 2026** to remain compliant with EU regulations. Non-compliance may result in market restrictions or penalties.

## Documentation Requirements
- Risk assessment report
- Technical documentation (including design documentation)
- Verification reports
- CE declaration of conformity
- Records of testing and validation

Customer segmentation is a strategic framework for dividing a market into distinct groups of customers with similar characteristics, needs, and behaviors. This knowledge card defines customer segments (also known as ICP - Ideal Customer Profile) with focus on firmographics and functional needs.

Key elements of a customer segment include:

1. **Firmographics** (demographic data):
   - Industry type (e.g., SaaS, manufacturing)
   - Company size (employee count, revenue range)
   - Geographic location (country, region, city)
   - Business structure (sole proprietor, corporation, etc.)
   - Technology stack (cloud infrastructure, CRM systems)

2. **Functional Needs** (pain points and requirements):
   - Operational challenges (e.g., supply chain inefficiencies)
   - Strategic goals (e.g., market expansion, product innovation)
   - Budget constraints (annual IT spending, ROI expectations)
   - Decision-making processes (purchase timelines, approval hierarchies)
   - Integration requirements (API compatibility, system interoperability)

3. **Behavioral Patterns** (usage and engagement):
   - Technology adoption lifecycle (early adopters vs. laggards)
   - Content consumption habits (blog readers, webinar attendees)
   - Channel preferences (email, LinkedIn, in-person events)
   - Customer support needs (self-service vs. premium support)

4. **Psychographic Factors** (attitudes and values):
   - Innovation readiness (willingness to try new technologies)
   - Risk tolerance (conservative vs. agile business approach)
   - Sustainability priorities (environmental, social, governance)
   - Cultural preferences (global vs. localized solutions)

This definition serves as a foundational artifact for market analysis, sales targeting, and product development. It enables data-driven decisions by creating clear boundaries between customer groups with distinct value propositions.

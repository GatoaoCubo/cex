# Sales Playbook: Commercial Conversation Protocol

## Strategic Frame

Sales is structured value delivery. Every conversation follows the same architecture: qualify fast (avoid wasting time on non-ICP), demonstrate ROI (not features), handle objections (preempt, not react), and close on value (not pressure). Strategic Greed is patient -- it closes deals where the ROI math is undeniable.

## ICP Qualification (MEDDIC Framework)

Before demo, qualify all 6 dimensions:

| Dimension | Question to Ask | Disqualify If |
|-----------|----------------|---------------|
| **M**etrics | "What does success look like in 90 days? How do you measure it?" | No measurable goal |
| **E**conomic Buyer | "Who signs off on tools like this?" | No access to economic buyer |
| **D**ecision Criteria | "What's most important to you: speed, quality, cost, compliance?" | Criteria CEX can't win |
| **D**ecision Process | "Walk me through how you'd evaluate this. What's the timeline?" | No process = no urgency |
| **I**dentify Pain | "What's the most painful part of how you do this today?" | Pain doesn't match N06 value |
| **C**hampion | "Who else on your team would benefit from seeing this?" | No internal advocate |

Score: 5-6 = hot lead (direct to demo). 3-4 = nurture. <3 = politely exit.

## Discovery Phase (15 minutes)

```
1. Context question (2 min):
   "Tell me about how you currently create [artifact type / brand materials / commercial content]."
   
2. Pain quantification (5 min):
   "How many hours per week does your team spend on this?"
   "What's the quality like? How many revision cycles?"
   "What's the cost of getting it wrong or slow?"
   
3. Desired state (5 min):
   "In an ideal world, what does this process look like?"
   "What would you do with the time you'd save?"
   
4. ROI setup (3 min):
   "Let me run some quick math with your numbers..."
   [Input into roi_calculator_n06 live during call]
```

## Demo Sequence (30 minutes)

```
Slide 1 -- Their Pain (2 min):
  "Based on what you told me: [reflect back their pain points]"
  "You're spending [X hours/week], which at [$Y/hr] is [$Z/month]"
  "That's [$Z*12] per year on a manual process."

Slide 2 -- CEX Positioning (3 min):
  "CEX is not a writing tool or a template library."
  "It's a typed enterprise brain -- 257 kinds of commercial knowledge, 8 reasoning steps per artifact."
  "The 8F pipeline is what makes a 5-word input produce a professional output."

Slide 3 -- ROI Proof (5 min):
  Show roi_calculator with their numbers.
  "Your ROI: [X]% in the first month. Payback: [N] days."
  "Even if we're only half right, you're still [net_value/2] ahead."

Slide 4 -- Live Demo (15 min):
  "Let me build something relevant to your exact use case right now."
  Pick the use case closest to their pain.
  Show: intent -> 8F pipeline -> artifact output (3 minutes)
  Show: iteration (change one thing, regenerate in seconds)

Slide 5 -- Plan + Pricing (5 min):
  "Based on your team size and use case, [TIER] is the fit."
  Show feature matrix for recommended tier vs the one below.
  "The step up from [tier-1] to [recommended] gets you [key feature they need]."
```

## Objection Handling

| Objection | Root Cause | Response |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| "We already use [ChatGPT / Jasper / Copy.ai]" | Not differentiated | "Those are generation tools. CEX is a typed knowledge system. The difference: those give you text. CEX gives you artifact types with quality gates, brand memory, and 8F reasoning. Let me show you the gap in 2 minutes." |
| "It's too expensive" | Value not proven | "Let's look at your math. At [$rate/hr] x [hours] -- you're spending [$X/month] on this manually. CEX is [$price/month]. The math says you're spending [$X] to avoid [$price]. Does that feel right?" |
| "We need to think about it" | Missing urgency | "What would help you decide faster? Is there a specific use case you'd want to test first?" + Offer 14-day pilot on most relevant use case |
| "Our team won't adopt it" | Change management fear | "What you're describing is a training problem, not a tool problem. We onboard [similar team size] in [hours]. Here's how..." + Show learning_record REW_002 onboarding checkpoint pattern |
| "We need IT/Legal approval" | Enterprise sales cycle | Switch to Enterprise flow. Schedule intro call with IT/Legal stakeholders. Provide security docs + data residency options. |
| "Can we get a discount?" | Price sensitivity | "I can offer annual pricing -- that's 2 months free, same as 17% off. Want me to run the annual numbers?" Do NOT offer ad hoc discounts outside of annual framing. |

## Closing Protocol

### Trial Close (use throughout demo):
"Does this solve the problem you described earlier?"
"Can you see your team using this for [their use case]?"

### Hard Close (end of demo):
```
"Based on everything we covered:
 - Your ROI is [X]% -- payback in [N] days
 - [TIER] plan solves the [specific pain they identified]
 - Setup takes [time], you'd be running by [date]

What would you need to move forward this week?"
```

### Next Steps Protocol:
- Hot lead: send checkout link in meeting + follow up in 24h
- Warm lead: send ROI one-pager + schedule follow-up in 3 days
- Needs approval: loop in economic buyer + send formal proposal

## Qualification Disqualifiers (Exit Politely)

```
PASS if any of:
- Team < 1 person (solo hobbyist, not commercial buyer)
- Budget < $50/month firm (below STARTER, no upgrade path)
- Timeline > 6 months (no urgency)
- Not the decision maker AND no champion identified
- Use case is pure content generation (better fit: Jasper, Copy.ai)
```

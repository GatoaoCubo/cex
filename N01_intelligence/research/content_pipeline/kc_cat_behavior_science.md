## 1. Stress Signals & Body Language

### Tail Position Decoder

| Position | Meaning | Content Angle |

| Straight up | Confident, happy, ready to engage | "Seu gato esta feliz? Olha o rabo!" |

**Source**: Tuft & Paw Definitive Guide; PAWS Chicago; PetMD; Cats Protection UK; Country Grove Veterinary Clinic

### Slow Blink Meaning

The slow blink is a feline trust signal -- equivalent to a "kiss" in cat communication. Cats use it to signal non-threat to other cats and humans. Reciprocating the slow blink builds trust.

**Content opportunity**: "Voce sabe fazer o 'beijo de gato'?" -- Reel demonstrating slow blink technique (high shareability, universal appeal).

**Source**: AAFP/ISFM Environmental Needs Guidelines 2013; dvm360; VCA

### Vertical Space

Cats perceive territory in 3D (floor to ceiling, 360 degrees -- Jackson Galaxy). Adding vertical elements effectively multiplies available territory without adding floor space. Key elements:

- Cat trees/shelves at 3+ heights
- Window perches (prey-watching enrichment)
- Elevated sleeping spots (safety + warmth)

**Content angle**: "Como triplicar o territorio do seu gato SEM mudar de apartamento" (carousel, before/after photos)

### Puzzle Feeders

NIH research: 85% of group-housed cats engaged with food puzzles without aggression increases. Benefits: mental stimulation, slower eating, reduced boredom, weight management.

**Content angle**: "DIY comedouro inteligente com rolo de papel higienico" (Reel, high save rate)

## 5. Sleep Patterns

### Key Data

| Metric | Value | Source |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Average sleep/day | 12-16 hours | Sleep Foundation |
| Kitten sleep | Up to 20 hours | welltayl.com |
| Senior cat sleep | Up to 20 hours | Union Lake Vet |
| Nap duration (avg) | 78 minutes | Sleep Foundation |
| Sleep pattern | Polyphasic (multiple naps) | PubMed |
| Chronotype | Crepuscular (dawn + dusk peaks) | Dr. Malou |
| Deep sleep % | ~25% of total sleep | Vet sources |

### Why Cats Sleep So Much

1. **Energy conservation**: Evolved from ambush predators who expend massive energy in short bursts
2. **Thermoregulation**: Sleep in warm spots to conserve metabolic energy
3. **Growth hormone**: Released during sleep (critical for kittens)
4. **Brain consolidation**: Memory processing during REM sleep

## 6. Nutrition Fundamentals

### Wet vs. Dry Food Comparison

| Factor | Wet Food | Dry Food | Verdict |
|--------|----------|----------|---------|
| Moisture | 75%+ | 6-10% | Wet wins for hydration |
| Protein | Higher, meat-based | Lower, needs starch binder | Wet wins for protein |
| Carbs | Lower | Higher (15-50%) | Wet wins for low-carb |
| Dental health | Minimal benefit | Slight mechanical cleaning | Draw (neither proven) |
| Convenience | Shorter shelf life | Long shelf life, easy serving | Dry wins for convenience |
| Cost | More expensive per calorie | Cheaper per calorie | Dry wins for budget |
| Kidney support | Excellent (hydration) | Risk factor (dehydration) | Wet wins significantly |

**Vet consensus**: Mix of wet + dry is optimal. AAFP reports wet food increases water intake ~20%.

### Toxic Foods (NEVER Feed)

| Toxic Food | Effect | Severity |
|------------|--------|----------|
| Chocolate (theobromine) | Heart arrhythmia, seizures, death | FATAL |
| Onion/garlic | Red blood cell destruction, anemia | SEVERE |
| Grapes/raisins | Acute kidney failure | SEVERE |
| Xylitol (sweetener) | Liver failure | FATAL |
| Alcohol | CNS depression, coma | FATAL |
| Raw eggs (avidin) | Biotin deficiency | MODERATE |
| Caffeine | Heart arrhythmia, restlessness | SEVERE |
| Lilies (all parts) | Acute kidney failure | FATAL in cats |

**Source**: ASPCA Poison Control; VCA; PMC/NIH; WebMD Pets

**Content opportunity**: "8 alimentos que MATAM gatos" (carousel, high save rate, annual reshare potential)

### Hydration Science

- Cats evolved from desert ancestors (Felis silvestris lybica) -- low thirst drive is genetic
- Water fountains increase consumption 20-50% (PMC study)
- Running water triggers evolutionary instinct: moving water = safer than stagnant
- Kidney disease is the #1 killer of senior cats -- hydration is the best prevention

## 7. Senior Cat Care (10+ years)

### Common Geriatric Issues

| Condition | Prevalence (12+ years) | Signs | Content Angle |
|-----------|----------------------|-------|---------------|
| Arthritis/DJD | 60%+ (often undiagnosed) | Reluctance to jump, stiffness, hiding | "Seu gato velho nao e preguicoso -- pode ter dor" |
| CKD (kidney disease) | 30-40% | Increased thirst, weight loss, vomiting | "Fontes de agua salvam rins" (product tie-in) |
| Cognitive Dysfunction (CDS) | 28% (11-14y), 50%+ (15+) | Night crying, disorientation, litter misses | "Demencia felina existe -- saiba identificar" |
| Hyperthyroidism | 10% of 10+ cats | Weight loss despite appetite, hyperactivity | Blog: "Emagrecendo mas comendo bem?" |
| Dental disease | 70%+ | Bad breath, drooling, reluctance to eat | "Fevereiro e mes da saude dental pet" |

### DISHA Acronym (CDS Signs)

| Letter | Sign | Observable Behavior |
|--------|------|-------------------|
| D | Disorientation | Staring at walls, getting lost in familiar rooms |
| I | Interaction changes | Withdrawal or unusual clinginess |
| S | Sleep-wake disruption | Night vocalization, sleeping all day |
| H | House-soiling | Forgetting litter box location |
| A | Activity changes | Decreased play, aimless wandering |

**Source**: Cornell Feline Health Center; International Cat Care (icatcare.org); Hill's Pet; Banfield

## 8. Kitten Development

### Critical Stages

| Stage | Age | Key Events | Content Format |
|-------|-----|------------|----------------|
| Neonatal | 0-2 weeks | Eyes closed, rely on mother, nurse every 2h | Educational carousel |
| Socialization (CRITICAL) | 2-7 weeks | Exposure window: humans, sounds, textures | Blog: "As 5 semanas mais importantes da vida do gato" |
| Extended socialization | 7-14 weeks | Play peaks at 12 weeks, social hierarchy forms | Reel: "Gatinhos aprendendo a ser gatos" |
| Adolescence | 3-6 months | Sexual maturity approaching, energy peak | "Castracao: QUANDO e POR QUEE" |
| Young adult | 6-18 months | Territory establishment, personality solidifies | "Meu gatinho virou gato adulto" |

### First Vet Visit Checklist

- Age 6-8 weeks (or ASAP after adoption)
- Core vaccines: FVRCP (feline viral rhinotracheitis, calicivirus, panleukopenia)
- Deworming protocol
- FeLV/FIV testing
- Flea prevention
- Spay/neuter discussion (typically 5-6 months)
- Microchip discussion

**Source**: Merck Veterinary Manual; Royal Canin Academy; Work It Kitty; Preventive Vet
